*post about the nonhuman characters of star trek who strive to be recognized about humans, and the way their stories explore various different aspects of identity. but also... theres something really interesting to me about the way it implicitly frames humans as the ideal beings, all other beings somehow inferior to humans. which to some extent makes sense. we're human. we only know humanity. the other races of star trek largely exist as caricatures that zoom in on specific flaws of humans today, while the humans of star trek are largely (at least intended) as an imagination of humans at their best.*
